# Must-be-true claims — Tesla Optimus robot (US + China) — multi-round debate
Date: 2026-06-05
Horizon: 1-3y

1. Tesla has primary-source disclosures indicating ongoing development/investment in autonomous robots and robot production lines (intent/capacity framing), even if near-term shipped volumes are not yet proven.
2. The report clearly states whether it is “map-first” vs “bottleneck-proven”, and does not overclaim demand, lead times, or supplier concentration without evidence.
3. A physical, end-to-end supply chain map exists (raw materials → components → modules → deployments), with at least one explicitly chosen “node under study”.
4. For the chosen node, the report defines measurable bottleneck tests (lead time/backlog/capex/yield/field failure signals) and lists what evidence is missing.
5. Supplier confirmation discipline is enforced: no company is labeled **Anchor-confirmed** without primary evidence (anchor filings, supplier filings, or joint PR).
6. Catalyst clock is time-bounded to dated reporting windows and has explicit go/no-go criteria.
7. Bear case/disconfirmers are explicit, measurable, and tied to monitoring signals.
