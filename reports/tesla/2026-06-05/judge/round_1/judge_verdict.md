## Rationale (summary)

This is a “map-first” research scaffold, not a Serenity chokepoint/bottleneck thesis: it does not satisfy the must-be-true set (demand is not confirmed by primary signals, the must-pass gate is not named/mechanized, supplier concentration is not established, >12m lead times are not evidenced, value-capture path is not tied to company-level numbers, and catalysts are not dated). ## Top 5 weakest claims (fix + required evidence channel)

1) “Demand confirmed”
- Fix: ≥2 independent primary demand signals: Tesla numeric deployment/production targets or commitments (Tesla IR deck / earnings transcript / SEC filing), plus customer-site evidence tied to units (regulatory filings, safety filings, contracts disclosed by counterparties).

2) “Must-pass gate exists (chokepoint)”
- Fix: name the exact gate (standard/cert/safety case/qualification) and its revalidation rules (standards body docs + certification registry + Tesla safety case disclosures); show it connects ≥2 downstream ecosystems, not only Tesla.

3) “Supplier count ≤3 (or switching time long)”
- Fix: node-by-node supplier map backed by primary confirmation (supplier annual report naming Tesla/program, joint PRs, import/export where applicable, teardown/BOM evidence with provenance) + qualification-time evidence.

4) “Lead time >12m”
- Fix: measurable constraints: lead-time quotes/backlog, tooling lead times, capacity/capex disclosures, procurement data; triangulate across ≥2 channels.

5) “Value capture visible in 4–8 quarters”
- Fix: an explicit accounting bridge: which line item moves and what disclosure will show it (segment revenue/backlog/GM/ASP), tied to named beneficiaries.

## Top 3 disconfirmers + monitoring signals

- Fast multi-sourcing / vertical integration kills supplier value capture
